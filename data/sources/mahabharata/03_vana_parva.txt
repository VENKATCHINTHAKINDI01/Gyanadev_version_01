MAHABHARATA
Book 3 — The Vana Parva (Forest Book)
Translated by Kisari Mohan Ganguli

1. After losing the dice game to the cunning Shakuni and Duryodhana, the five Pandavas were forced to undergo twelve years of forest exile and one year of living in disguise among ordinary people.

2. Yudhishthira, the eldest Pandava, accepted the exile with grace and patience. He said: A man of wisdom and virtue must always honour his word, for dharma is the foundation of all life.

3. Bhima was filled with burning rage against Duryodhana and Shakuni who had cheated them. But he restrained himself at Yudhishthira's wise counsel, though his grief was like fire within him.

4. Draupadi wept bitterly in the forest and spoke harsh words: Tell me, O Yudhishthira, how can a righteous man like you suffer such injustice at the hands of these wicked and deceitful men?

5. Arjuna, knowing that the Pandavas would need divine weapons for the future battle, went on a great journey to obtain them. He performed severe austerities and pleased the gods with his devotion.

6. Lord Shiva, disguised as a hunter, fought with Arjuna. Recognising his student's courage, Shiva revealed himself and gave Arjuna the Pashupatastra, the most powerful weapon in existence, capable of destroying the entire universe if used carelessly.

7. Indra, king of the gods and Arjuna's divine father, gave Arjuna a home in heaven and taught him the use of the divine weapons. Arjuna spent five years in the heavens learning these celestial arts.

8. During the exile, the sage Markandeya came to the Pandavas and told them the story of Rama and Sita, to give them comfort and courage in their time of suffering.

9. Yudhishthira asked the sage: Why do the righteous suffer while the wicked prosper? The sage answered: Suffering is the test of virtue. Gold is purified by fire; the virtuous are purified by hardship.

10. Krishna visited the Pandavas in the forest and promised them: Justice will prevail in the end. The cause of dharma is never truly lost. The wicked may prosper for a time, but righteousness always triumphs.

11. In the twelfth year of their exile, the Pandavas prepared to enter the one year of disguise. They went to the kingdom of Virata and lived there incognito, performing their year of disguise successfully.

12. When the year of disguise was complete and the Kauravas discovered the Pandavas, it was clear that war was now inevitable. The Pandavas demanded the return of their kingdom according to the agreement.
